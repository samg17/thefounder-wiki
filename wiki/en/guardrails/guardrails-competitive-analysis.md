# Guardrails — Competitive analysis

Mistakes founders make when mapping competitors and defining positioning.

---

## Mapping competitors

❌ Listing only the obvious direct competitors ("our competitors are A, B and C")
✅ Also map: (1) alternatives the customer uses today even if imperfect, (2) the status quo ("do nothing" or "do it manually"), (3) indirect competitors solving the same job

❌ Ignoring homemade solutions and workarounds
✅ The workaround the customer uses today IS your biggest competitor — and it tells you how much pain they can tolerate

❌ Comparing features instead of outcomes
✅ The customer doesn't buy features — they buy progress on some dimension. Compare outcomes delivered

---

## Evaluating threats

❌ Underestimating large competitors ("they're slow, they'll take a long time to react")
✅ Slow large players still have distribution, brand and capital. Assess what they'd do if you gained traction

❌ Overestimating technology barriers ("our tech is hard to copy")
✅ Most technology barriers last 12–18 months. The real moat comes from data, network effects or switching costs

❌ Not monitoring competitors systematically
✅ Set a review frequency (monthly) and what to monitor: pricing, positioning, hiring, product

---

## Positioning

❌ Generic positioning ("we're faster, cheaper and easier")
✅ Positioning is a choice — being better for a specific segment, not for everyone

❌ Defining positioning without anchoring to the ICP
✅ For whom specifically are you the obvious choice? Why? See [[icp]]

❌ Copying the market leader's positioning
✅ The leader already owns that space. Find the angle they can't or won't occupy

❌ Positioning based on a feature the competitor can copy in weeks
✅ Sustainable positioning comes from: segment served, business model, distribution or unique data

---

## Moats and competitive advantages

❌ Believing "proprietary technology" is a sufficient moat
✅ Technology alone is rarely a moat. Evaluate: network effects, switching costs, data, scale, brand

❌ Confusing short-term advantage with moat
✅ Moat = advantage that strengthens over time and growth, not one that dilutes

❌ Not articulating why the moat is hard to replicate
✅ Be able to answer: "why in 3 years can't a well-funded competitor copy us?"

---

## Judgment errors

❌ "We have no competitors" — there's always an alternative, even if it's the status quo
✅ If you truly have no competitors, question whether the problem really exists

❌ Superficial benchmarking — only analyzing the competitor's website and pricing
✅ Use the product, interview their customers, read reviews, talk to ex-employees

❌ Competitor obsession — spending more energy on competition than on customers
✅ The best founders are obsessed with the customer's problem, not the competitor
